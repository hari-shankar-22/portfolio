SUPER MARKET SALES ANALYSIS WITH POWER-BI

INTRODUCTION

This project focuses on exploring and visualizing a supermarket sales dataset that contains extensive records of sales, profit, revenue,
taxes, customer information, and store details.
The primary goal is to gain insights into various aspects of sales, payment, tax, quantity, and profit. 
The analysis employs various visualizations, including total metrics, distribution analyses using pie charts, bar graphs, and 
a detailed table providing comprehensive information about products.


Dataset Overview:

Dataset Name: Supermarket Sales
Attributes: Sales, Profit, Revenue, Taxes, Customer Information, Stores Information
Dataset Source: Kaggle


Visualizations and Analysis:

1. Total Metrics Analysis:

Total Sales: Sum of all sales recorded in the dataset.
Total Payment: Sum of all payment transactions.
Total Tax: Cumulative tax amount across all records.
Total Quantity: Aggregate quantity of products sold.
Total Profit: Cumulative profit derived from sales.

2. Distribution Analysis using Pie Charts:
 a. Sales by Gender:
       A pie chart depicting the distribution of sales based on the gender of customers.

  b. Sales by City:
      A pie chart showcasing the distribution of sales across different cities.

  c. Sales by Branch:
      A pie chart illustrating the proportion of sales contributed by each branch.

3. Sales by Profit by City (Bar Graph):
  A bar graph demonstrating the correlation between sales and profit across different cities.
  This visualization helps identify cities with high-profit margins.

4. Product Information Table:
  A comprehensive table containing detailed information about each product. The table includes:

    Product details
    Store information
    Invoice ID
    Total revenue generated
    Total profit earned


CONCLUSION

The visualizations and analyses conducted in this project provide a deep understanding of various facets of the supermarket sales dataset.
Insights into total metrics, distribution by gender, city, and branch, as well as the correlation between sales and profit, 
contribute to informed decision-making and strategy development.
This project aids stakeholders in identifying patterns, making data-driven decisions, 
and optimizing various aspects of supermarket operations. 
The detailed product information table offers a valuable resource for understanding the performance of individual products across 
different stores.
The findings from this analysis can be used to enhance marketing strategies, optimize inventory, and improve overall business efficiency.
